The Blue Devils

Founded in 1957, the Blue Devils have been a staple of excellence and achievement in the marching community. They won their first championship in 1976 and havenâ€™t looked back since, winning 20 more titles to date - the most in Drum Corps International history. The Blue Devils quickly rose to prominence, and made themselves at home at the top of the rankings.


Since 1975, they have placed no lower than fifth at DCI Championships, and since 2007 they have either finished first or second place. The Blue Devils hold the record for the highest score ever received at DCI World Championships, with an astonishing 99.650 awarded to their 2014 program, Felliniesque. This score is half a point higher than the next highest score at 99.15 (Cavaliers 2002, Cadets 2005).


In addition to this peak score, the Blue Devils remain the only corps in Drum Corps history to score above 99 points twice. The blue Devils joined the Cadets and Cavaliers in the three-peat club at the conclusion of the 2023 DCI World Championships, where they won their third consecutive Gold Medal for the years 2019, 2022, and 2023 (2020 was canceled due to COVID-19, and 2021 was a non-competitive year).


Like many Drum Corps, the Blue Devils began as drum and flag units and slowly evolved with the activity into the full drum and bugle corps they are today. Today, the Blue Devils are an exceptional example of financial stability and stewardship in the marching world, and serve as trailblazers when it comes to economic advancement of Drum Corps International.
